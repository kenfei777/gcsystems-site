B — Craft & Process

How We Handle Every Harvest

Terpene integrity is fragile and irreplaceable. Our post-harvest process is built around one principle: preserve what the plant produced.

01

Hang-Dried

Every harvest is hung whole and cold-dried in temperature-controlled rooms at under 18°C. Lower temperature slows terpene volatilisation during the drying stage — preserving the aromatic and therapeutic compounds that distinguish medical-grade product from standard commercial supply.

Cold-dried Under 18°C Terpene-preserving
02

Slow-Cured

Flower is slow-cured for 4–8 weeks in humidity-controlled sealed vessels — longer than industry standard. An extended cure stabilises moisture content, allows cannabinoid and terpene profiles to fully develop, and produces a product that performs consistently batch after batch.

4–8 weeks Humidity-controlled Profile stability
03

Hand-Trimmed & Selected

All flower is trimmed and selected by hand. Machine trimming is faster — but the mechanical action damages the resin glands that hold cannabinoids and terpenes. Hand-trimming is the only method that preserves full potency and terpene expression in the final product.

Hand-selected Resin intact Premium grade
04

Always Fresh

Bi-weekly small-batch rotational cultivation means we're always harvesting. Every shipment is recently cured and freshly dispatched — what you receive was processed recently, and it shows in the product.

Bi-weekly harvests Year-round production Fresh to market
05

Clean Product

No pesticide, herbicide, fungicide or PGR used during the entire cultivation process.

Zero Pesticide Zero PGR Batch tested by GMP Lab
